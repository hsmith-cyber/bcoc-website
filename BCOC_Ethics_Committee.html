Purpose

Why this Committee exists, and what it is charged with doing.

The Black Chief Officers Committee (BCOC) is governed by its Bylaws (v 2017) and its Code of Ethics (approved in 2025), which together establish the standards of conduct for officers and board members and the procedures for addressing alleged violations of those standards.

The BCOC Code of Ethics provides that "infractions of this Statement of Personal and Professional Standards of Conduct are to be reported directly to any member of the audit committee who shall, in their determination, bring the infraction to the full executive committee."

Because no permanent audit committee is presently constituted, the Chair has established this Ad Hoc Ethics Review Committee to stand in for the audit-committee function described in the Code of Ethics until a permanent committee is duly constituted. The Chair is authorized under the Bylaws to appoint standing and ad hoc committees to carry out the functions of the organization and to address matters requiring independent review.

The receipt, evaluation, and disposition of ethics-related complaints is a function essential to the integrity of the BCOC and cannot be suspended pending the formation of a permanent audit committee. Sound governance practice requires that the body receiving, investigating, and evaluating an ethics complaint be separate and distinct from the body ultimately deciding any disciplinary action — preserving the impartiality of the deciding body and the procedural fairness owed to any officer or board member who is the subject of a complaint.

Charge & Authority

What the Committee is empowered to do — and the boundaries of that authority.

The Committee is charged to:

  • Receive written complaints alleging violations of the BCOC Code of Ethics by officers, board members, or members of the BCOC.
  • Review the evidence submitted, request additional documentary evidence as necessary, and receive written or oral statements from the complainant, the subject of the complaint, and any material witnesses.
  • Provide the subject of the complaint with written notice of the allegations, copies of the supporting evidence, and a reasonable opportunity to respond in writing and, if requested, to appear before the Committee.
  • Make written findings of fact based on the evidence considered.
  • Prepare a written report and recommendation to the Executive Committee — which may include dismissing the complaint, issuing a written warning, requiring remedial action, or referring the matter for consideration of suspension, removal, or other authorized disciplinary action.

Limitations on Authority

The authority of the Committee is advisory and recommendatory. The Committee shall have no authority to impose discipline, to remove any officer or board member from office, or to alter the membership status of any individual.

Final authority on all disciplinary matters rests with the Executive Committee or the full Board of the BCOC, as provided in the Bylaws.

Non-voting Executive Board members serving on the Committee shall not, by reason of such service, acquire any voting authority on the Committee's recommendation when it is considered by the Executive Committee or the Board.

Conflicts of Interest & Counsel

Conflict-of-Interest Disclosure

No person shall serve on the Committee who has a personal, professional, familial, or business relationship with any individual whose conduct is the subject of a complaint pending before the Committee.

Upon the filing of any complaint, each appointed member shall disclose in writing any actual or potential conflict of interest. Any member with such a conflict shall be recused and, if necessary, temporarily replaced by a qualified alternate appointed by the Chair.

Engagement of Counsel

The Committee is authorized to request, through the Chair, the assistance of legal counsel retained by the BCOC on any matter of procedural or substantive law arising in the course of its work.

Any communication between the Committee and such counsel shall be treated as privileged.

Term & Dissolution

The Committee remains in existence until (i) a permanent audit committee is constituted, (ii) all referred matters are completed and the Chair determines no further ad hoc review is required, or (iii) this Resolution is rescinded by the Chair or the Board.
