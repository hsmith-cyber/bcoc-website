Code of Ethics

Integrity · Professionalism · Public Trust
A shared standard of conduct for the members and Board of the Black Chief Officers Committee — protecting the integrity of the organization and the communities its members serve.
All Members Officers & Board On & Off Duty Reportable

Purpose & Pledge

Why this Code exists and what every member commits to upholding.

The Black Chief Officers Committee (BCOC) primarily represents current and aspiring Black executives in the "All Hazards" response community. Trust is a staple of our organization. To ensure the continuing integrity of the BCOC, the highest standards of ethical conduct must be maintained at all times.

The purpose of this Professional Code of Ethics is to establish criteria that encourages members to promote a culture of ethical integrity and high standards of professionalism in our field. The broad scope of this Code is intended to mitigate and negate situations that may result in embarrassment and waning support for what has historically been a highly respected organization.

All members of the BCOC share a responsibility to project an ethical character of professionalism, integrity, compassion, loyalty, and honesty in all that we do, all of the time. Doing so assists in fostering a positive public perception.

Members Shall

The standards of conduct every BCOC member is expected to uphold.

1

Always conduct themselves, on and off duty, in a manner that reflects positively on self, family, and the BCOC.

2

Accept responsibility for their actions and for the consequences of their actions.

3

Support the concept of fairness and the value of diverse thoughts and opinions.

4

Avoid situations that would adversely affect the credibility or public perception of the BCOC.

5

Are truthful and honest at all times and report instances of cheating or other dishonest or unethical acts that compromise the integrity of the BCOC.

6

Conduct personal affairs in a manner that does not improperly influence the performance of their duties or bring discredit to the BCOC.

7

Are respectful and conscious of each member's safety and welfare.

8

Recognize that they serve in a position of public trust that requires stewardship in the honest and efficient use of human resources, fiscal resources, and all publicly owned resources, and that these resources are protected from misuse and theft.

9

Exercise professionalism, competence, respect, and loyalty in the performance of their duties and use information, confidential or otherwise, gained by virtue of their positions, only to benefit those they are entrusted to serve.

10

Avoid financial investments, outside employment, outside business interests, or activities that conflict with — or are enhanced by — their official positions, or have the potential to create the perception of impropriety.

11

Never propose or accept personal rewards, special privileges, benefits, advancement, honors, or gifts that may create a conflict of interest or the appearance thereof.

12

Never discriminate on the basis of race, religion, color, creed, age, marital status, national origin, ancestry, gender, sexual preference, medical condition, or disability.

13

Never harass, intimidate, threaten, or bully — in any manner — fellow members of the BCOC, the fire service, and/or the public; and shall stop and/or report the actions of others who engage in such behaviors.

14

Comply with all laws and campaign rules when supporting political candidates and engaging in political activities.

15

Refrain from endorsing commercial products through quotations, use of photographs, and testimonials for personal gain.

16

Responsibly use social networking, electronic communications, or other media technology in a manner that does not discredit, dishonor, or embarrass the BCOC. Members also understand that failure to resolve or report inappropriate use of these media equates to condoning such behavior.

Officers & Board Members

Heightened standards for those entrusted with the leadership of the organization.

All officers and Board members of the BCOC are required to exercise the highest ethical standards of conduct and practice fundamental honesty at all times.

Officers and Board Members Shall Not
  • Deceive, defraud, or mislead BCOC members, officers, or those with whom the BCOC has business or other relationships.
  • Misrepresent the BCOC in any negotiations, dealings, contracts, or agreements.
  • Divulge or release any information relating to the BCOC's plans, mission, or operational databases without appropriate approval.
  • Obtain a personal advantage or benefit by use of the organization's name.
  • Accept individual gifts of any kind in excess of $[amount to be set] in connection with the board member's relationship with the BCOC.
  • Withhold their best efforts to perform their duties to acceptable standards.
  • Engage in unethical business practices of any type.
  • Use BCOC property, financial resources, or services of BCOC members for personal benefit.
  • Be convicted of any civil or criminal offense(s).

Reporting Infractions

Infractions of this Statement of Personal and Professional Standards of Conduct are to be reported directly to any member of the Audit Committee, who shall, in their determination, bring the infraction to the full Executive Committee.

* This Professional Code of Ethics is adopted by the Black Chief Officers Committee to articulate the shared standards of conduct expected of every member and officer. It is intended to complement — not replace — applicable laws, employer rules of conduct, and any sworn oaths of office that BCOC members may hold in their primary roles.
