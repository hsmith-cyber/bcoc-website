Purpose

What the Committee exists to do, and how it operates.

The Bylaws Committee is a working body of the Office of the Chair, charged with the orderly review, drafting, and stewardship of proposed amendments to the BCOC Bylaws in accordance with Article X of the BCOC Bylaws.

The Committee operates as a working body of the Chair and reports through the Chair to the Executive Board and the membership.

The Committee opens a member comment period on its initial draft, integrates feedback received, and transmits a final draft to the Executive Board for adoption consideration. Adoption authority rests with the Executive Board and the membership under Article X — the Committee’s role is to prepare, recommend, and present.

Charge & Authority

What the Committee is empowered to do — and where its authority ends.

The Committee is charged to:

  • Review the BCOC Bylaws and identify provisions warranting amendment, clarification, or modernization.
  • Draft proposed amendments in redline or blue-font form and circulate them to the membership through the Chair of the BCOC.
  • Open and administer a member comment period and incorporate received feedback into a final draft.
  • Transmit the final draft to the Executive Board through the Chair of the BCOC, with the Committee’s recommendation.
  • Assist with and present the key changes to the membership at the BCOC Annual Conference prior to the membership vote.

Limitations on Authority

The Committee is a working body with recommending authority. It does not adopt, ratify, or finalize any Bylaws amendment.

Adoption authority for proposed Bylaws amendments rests with the Executive Board and the membership in accordance with Article X of the BCOC Bylaws.

Nothing in the Committee’s operating protocols modifies, supersedes, or waives any provision of the BCOC Bylaws, the BCOC Code of Ethics, or any standing policy of the BCOC.

Recusals & Counsel

Mandatory Recusal — Chief Thomas

Battalion Chief (Ret.) Levi Thomas serves on both the BCOC Executive Board and the Bylaws Committee. To preserve the integrity of the process and avoid any appearance of self-review, Chief Thomas shall recuse himself from Executive Board deliberations and votes on Bylaws Committee work product.

Chief Thomas retains full voting rights within the Committee. The recusal applies only at the Executive Board level and is lifted upon completion of the applicable amendment cycle.

General standard: Any member who becomes aware of a personal, professional, or institutional interest that may reasonably call impartiality into question shall disclose it and recuse from deliberations and votes on that specific item.

Engagement of Counsel

Where the Committee identifies a question warranting legal or parliamentary review, the Chair of the Committee shall refer the question to the Chair of the BCOC, who may engage counsel or the Parliamentarian as appropriate.

Term & Dissolution

The Committee remains in effect until superseded by another resolution of the Chair or the Executive Board. Members serve at the pleasure of the Chair; vacancies are filled by the Chair at any time.
