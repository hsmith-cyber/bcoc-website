Article I: Name Adopted 1993

Current 2017 LanguageProposed 2026 Language
The organization is a standing committee of the International Association of Black Professional Firefighters (IABPFF) and shall be named the Black Chief Officers' Committee, hereafter referred to as "BCOC". No change.

Article II: Purpose Adopted 1993

Current 2017 LanguageProposed 2026 Language
The "BCOC" is a membership organization formed to provide unique resources to Black Fire Executives to enhance their ability to better serve the public and the firefighting profession. The BCOC shall exercise leadership in the development of minorities in public and private management positions; sponsor forums for discussion of timely issues and topical concerns; support the development and maintenance of a repository of Black Executive talent for job openings and technical assistance. No change.
Section I: Directives
a) To serve as a resource to the members of the IABPFF and assist in providing mentoring to the organization's leadership.No change.
b) To assist our Black brothers and sisters in securing appointments as chief officers as well as other advancements within the fire service.No change.
c) To represent the IABPFF at all Chief Officer functions when appropriate and necessary.No change.
d) To promote unity between Chief Officers and other members of the IABPFF.No change.
e) To continue to be active participants within the IABPFF.No change.
f) To provide a viable and valuable conduit between the IABPFF and the IAFC.No change.
Section II: Functions
a) To identify and respond to issues within the fire service that affect Black firefighters and Black Communities, which merit attention, inquiry or investigation.No change.
b) To provide a forum for discussion of issues which affect Black firefighters and the fire service.No change.
c) To develop recommendations for corrective action to resolve issues that affect Black firefighters.No change.
d) To provide resources which enhance the upward mobility of Blacks in the fire service.No change.
e) To develop an effective network of information on promotional opportunities for Black Firefighters.No change.

Article III: Membership Adopted 1993; Amended 2015

Current 2017 LanguageProposed 2026 Language
Membership is open to any person holding the rank of Chief Officer in a paid fire organization, and a member of a local affiliate or independent member of the IABPFF (if one exists) and is financially current with regards to applicable dues. No change.
Section 1: Categories
a) Individual Membership: Open to all Chief Fire Officers who subscribe to the goals and objectives of the organization.No change.
b) Associate Membership: Open to all professional associations, organizations and institutions, public and private, which subscribe to the goals and objectives of the BCOC and especially wish to help promote equal opportunities for all minorities.No change.
c) Corporate Membership: Open to all private organizations, which subscribe to the goals and objectives of the BCOC.No change.
e) Retired Membership: Open to any BCOC member who, at the time of separation, without prejudice, from a paid fire organization, was a member in good standing, is eligible for retired membership.No change.
f) Affiliate Membership: Open to all non-chief officers of fire rescue, EMS and communications department personnel who subscribe to the goals and objectives of the organization.No change.
g) Only Individual and Retired Membership are eligible to vote.No change.

Article V: Election Adopted 1993; Amended 2015, 2017; Proposed 2026

Current 2017 LanguageProposed 2026 Language
Section 1: Time and Place
a) Elections of the Chairperson, Recording Secretary, Financial Secretary, and Parliamentarian shall take place in odd years.No change.
b) Elections of the Vice Chairperson, Treasurer and Correspondence Secretary shall take place in even years.No change.
c) Staggered odd and even elections shall become effective commencing with the 2017 Business year.No change.
Section 2: Mode of Election
a) The election of the officers of the BCOC shall be by blind ballot after direct nomination. The nominee receiving the highest number of votes shall be declared elected by the presiding officers.No change.
b) If competing candidates receive an equal number of votes thereby creating a tie vote situation, the Executive Board will choose the winner on a majority vote of the Executive Board. This vote will be announced by the Chairperson.No change.
c) The Elections Committee will certify the "official results". "Official Results" of the vote will be presented to the membership. The candidate receiving the highest number of votes shall be elected to office.No change.
Section 3: Term of Office
a) The term of office of the officers of the BCOC shall begin at the close of the regular annual session of the BCOC Meeting in which they were elected.No change.
b) Term of office for elected officers shall be limited to two (2) consecutive terms.No change.
Section 4: Voting (Qualifications and Balloting)
Items 1 through 8 establish qualifications for receiving a ballot (good standing, registration, roll-call distribution, one vote per member, candidate profile submission 30 days before the annual meeting, ratification of single candidates, election process for two or more candidates, and Elections Committee integrity duties).No change.
9. Absentee ballots will be sent to members in good standing following a request by mail or electronically to the Elections Committee stating their name, address and email contact information. Requests must be received at least 45 days before the annual meeting.Revised9. Absentee ballots will be sent to members in good standing following a request by mail or electronically to the Elections Committee stating their name, address and email contact information. Requests must be received at least 30 days before the annual meeting.
Items 10 through 17 address confirmation of absentee requests, return deadlines, proxy ballot dissemination and handling, official mail results, floor nominations, and filling vacant offices.No change.
Section 5: Removal of Officers
a) The BCOC Executive Board may recommend to the BCOC membership that an officer be removed for failure to perform his/her duties. Such removal requires a two-thirds affirmative vote of both the BCOC Executive Board and or the BCOC membership.No change.
b) Any officer of the BCOC may resign by filing (timely) written notice with the Executive Board.No change.
c) Following removal or resignation of an officer, the Chairperson (or Vice Chairperson in the case of the Chairperson's removal or resignation) shall appoint a member to serve the remaining term of the vacated position.No change.
Section 6: Eligibility for Elected Office
a) To be eligible for any elected office, a candidate must have been an individual or Retired member in good standing for at least three (3) consecutive years. Service must have been immediately prior to announcement of intention to run for office.No change.
b) Those desiring to run for office must be financial and in good standing at least thirty (30) days prior to the start of the Annual Conference. Members must be financially current prior to the start of the Annual Conference in order to be eligible to vote in elections.No change.

Article IX: Conduct of Meetings Adopted 1993; Proposed 2026

Current 2017 LanguageProposed 2026 Language
Subsections (a) through (g): Existing Rules
a) Rules of Order: The presiding officer shall preserve order and decorum and shall take no part in debates while presiding. All questions of order shall be subject to an appeal to the conference, and upon such an appeal the vote shall be taken without debate. The presiding officer may state the reasons for the decision given and shall put the question as follows, "Shall the decision of the chair be sustained?" A two-thirds majority of those present and voting shall be necessary to reverse the decision of the chair.No change.
b) Speaking: Every member when speaking or offering a motion shall rise, state his or her name, their City and State and respectfully address the presiding officer, and when finished speaking shall at once resume his or her seat.No change.
c) Presiding Officer Duties: When two (2) or more members rise to speak at the same time, the presiding officer shall decide who is entitled to the floor.No change.
d) Point of Order: A member called to order shall immediately cease speaking and resume his or her seat until the point of order in question has been decided, when he or she shall again be entitled to the floor.No change.
e) Calling Questions: A motion to take the previous question shall always be in order except when a member is in possession of the floor, and must be put forward without debate and if supported by a majority vote of those voting, the motion shall be declared carried and no further debate or amendments shall be in order until the main question shall be decided.No change.
f) Motions to Adjourn: A motion to adjourn shall always be in order, except when a member is in possession of the floor or a vote is being taken or it has been decided that a vote shall now be taken. A motion to adjourn is not debatable, but a motion to adjourn to a given time is open to debate.No change.
g) Questions without Provision: The rules contained in the current edition of Robert's Rules of Order shall provide guidance for the organization in all cases to which they are applicable and in which they are not inconsistent with this constitution and by-laws and any special rules of order the organization may adopt.No change.
Subsection (h): Chair Discretion on Motions (New)
No provision.Newh) Chair Discretion on Motions: The presiding officer may decline to entertain a motion that is dilatory, frivolous, or manifestly designed to obstruct the orderly conduct of business. The presiding officer may also decline to entertain a motion that: (i) is substantially identical to a motion already defeated at the same meeting; (ii) conflicts with an existing standing rule, policy, or resolution of the Executive Board that has not been rescinded; or (iii) would require action that is outside the authority of the organization or its officers as defined by these bylaws. When the presiding officer declines to entertain a motion, the officer shall briefly state the reason for the ruling. Any such ruling is subject to appeal consistent with Article IX, Section (a). The Parliamentarian shall advise the presiding officer on all rulings made under this section.
