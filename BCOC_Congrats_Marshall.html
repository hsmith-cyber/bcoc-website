Rodney Marshall
Commissioner, Boston Fire Dept.
Historic Appointment

The Black Chief Officers Committee proudly congratulates Rodney Marshall on his appointment as the 45th Commissioner of the Boston Fire Department by Mayor Michelle Wu on April 16, 2026.

Commissioner Marshall makes history as the first Black fire commissioner in the department's nearly 350-year existence. A Dorchester native and 35-year veteran of the department, his rise from firefighter to Chief of Operations exemplifies the leadership, dedication, and excellence that BCOC champions in the fire service.

  • Background Dorchester native (Grove Hall), graduate of Boston College
  • Career 35-year veteran, joined in 1991, rose from firefighter to Chief of Operations
  • Achievements Designed and launched the department's Cadet Program creating career pathways for inner-city youth; two Commissioner's Unit Citations for bravery
  • Community Pledged to keep all firehouse doors open during summer months so neighborhood children can see equipment, meet firefighters, and be inspired
